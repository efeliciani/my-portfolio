Festinerary

A UX approach to itinerary building for large festivals and events. Simplifying the event planning process for festival goers.

Overview

Festinerary is an application designed to organize large quantities of information that come with attending large events like music festivals. The app builds personalized itineraries for users,  with intuitive navigation and easy accessibility.  

This is a personal project I took on inspired by a group assignment I completed in the Humber UXD certificate program. Originally, our prospective clients, North by North East music festival, requested we build a directory application for festival goers. Inspired by this idea, I decided to design my own directory application for large events who host multiple venues in one night. I used the NXNE as the example festival in my designs, in hopes they may be future clients interested in purchasing the application.

Problem

The process of planning an all-day, multiple day event can be tedious overwhelming. Festivals goers need to consider multiple variables when planning their experience, like how much time is needed to allocate to commute between stages. Currently, no tool exists to simplify the planning experience of a large event. Users benefit from a tool that will filter large quantities of information into an organized space and customize a personalized plan.
How might we help festival goers plan a seamless festival experience before, during and after the event?

Solution

1. Present information using a strategically designed information hierarchy

2. Create a structured filtration feature to customize a personal itinerary
‍
3. Store desired information in an easily accessible part of the application for users to reference
01. User Surveys

Finding the information users need.

First, I surveyed 20 participants who identified as frequent festival goers to find out how they navigate through a festival experience. I discovered what information users look for and prioritize most.  
Primary Questions:
  1. What makes a good festival experience?
  2. What important information do you need before, during and
    after and event?
  3. What information is most important for you to know?
  4. What information is least important for you to know?

02. Affinity mapping and Site mapping

Organizing the information into a hierarchy.

Second, I analyzed each participant’s answer and grouped like terms in an affinity map. Using these terms, I built an site map to visualize an information hierarchy.
03. User Persona and User Flow

Making a hypothetical scenario.

I created a persona based on the participants of my study and the original demographics NXNE provided Humber college. I came up with a simple user flow with a goal of building an itinerary and buying a ticket to guide the navigation of my frames.
This user flow focuses on the journey of making an itinerary for the NXNE music festival. The user opens the application and browses upcoming festivals. They click on the NXNE and browse their page. The user clicks "build festinerary" where they are brought to a festinerary builder page. This page allows them to browse through artists they'd like to see and add the artists to an itinerary. The user can click through the builder page and itinerary page to check on their progress. Once completed, they can purchase their ticket through the itinerary page.
04. Sketches and Wireframes

Exploring a visual navigation.

I focused on sketching an itinerary builder page with a filter, and an itinerary display page as these frames are the MVP (minimum viable feature) for my application.
Trying to concept an order for the builder and filter page based on my anaylsis results: date, venue, vendor, time, map, genre.
I drafted the same order for the NXNE Itinerary page. I want the itinerary to showcase the information in an easy-to-read format.

06. High Fidelity Prototypes

Putting designs together with a visual look and feel

Finally, I came up with a colour scheme of neon pinks and cool purples. I wanted the application to feel vibrant and exciting to match the excitement of a large festival.  The tab bar has 3 sections: a home tab to find upcoming festivals, a ticket tab to store all tickets with QR codes, and a festinaries tab to store all the itineraries the user has created.
The itinerary builder page is an essential page for the user's navigation. It was build with the information hierarchy determined in my analysis. The typography follows the same hierarchy: date, venue, vendor, artist, time, sound. Spacing is strategically used to intuitively communicate information to the user. For example, vendors are nested under venue names to show each venue hosts different companies.

The NXNE festinerary page shows is organized in the same hierarchy gives the user the location of each venue. The festinerary page will have an API integration linked to google maps, giving the user a sense for commute times between shows.
